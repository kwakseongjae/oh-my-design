## Systemic finding

1. **UK has world-class public design systems.** GOV.UK Design System (design-system.service.gov.uk — accessibility-law-mandated, gold standard), BBC **GEL**, Skyscanner **Backpack** (open-source, authoritative CSS token files on GitHub/unpkg) all gave token-authoritative extraction. Plus refero entries for monzo and farfetch. Best DS-surface availability after the US batch.
2. **Akamai/Cloudflare bot-walls + KR-IP geo-blocks were the recurring obstacle.** ASOS and Skyscanner (.net) blocked headless → headed `channel:'chrome'`; Skyscanner succeeded via `.co.kr` redirect; Deliveroo `.co.uk` was Cloudflare-geo-blocked from KR entirely, so tokens came from brand-owned `deliveroo.design` + Deliveroo Design Medium + Backpack-style sources. All honestly noted in `.verification.md`.
3. **Monochrome-as-luxury repeats.** Farfetch (Carbon `#222222`, 0px radius) and ASOS (`#2d2d2d`) use total decoration-absence as the brand signal — same pattern as Squarespace (US) and the KR automotive pair.
4. **Brand accent ≠ CTA fill.** Monzo's Hot Coral `#ff4f40` is accent-only (CTAs are Midnight Ink/Teal); GOV.UK's `#ffdd00` is the focus-ring, not a fill. Live inspect corrected the "obvious" brand-color-as-button assumption twice.

## Known shared limitation

KR-IP geo-blocking and Akamai/Cloudflare walls hit UK consumer commerce/delivery hardest (Deliveroo fully blocked, ASOS/Skyscanner headless-blocked). Where the live consumer site was unreachable, extraction fell back to **brand-owned design surfaces** (deliveroo.design, skyscanner.design/Backpack) — which for UK brands are unusually rich and authoritative, so quality held. Process note: for UK brands, probe `<brand>.design` and open-source token repos (Backpack on GitHub/unpkg) early — they often beat the geo-walled consumer site.
