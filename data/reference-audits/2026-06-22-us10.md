## Systemic finding

1. **US brands DO surface in Tier-2 / public design systems** — unlike KR/TW (0/10), this batch found refero entries for asana, squarespace, hubspot (3/10) and authoritative **public brand/DS portals** for several: HubSpot **Canvas** (canvas.hubspot.com, 200), Databricks **brand portal** (brand.databricks.com, 200), Asana **/brand**, and Reddit **RPL** tokens exposed live as 360+ CSS custom properties on `:root`. US tokens are the best-grounded of the four-country round.
2. **2022–2026 rebrands dominate.** PayPal (2023, PayPal Pro font, midnight `#002991` + sky `#60cdff`), Instacart (2022, carrot/kale), Asana (2026 monochrome pivot + magenta hero `#fd3ffd`), Reddit (Reddit Sans + RPL). Live inspect caught the current state, not the legacy palette training data would suggest.
3. **Cloudflare/bot protection needed headed Chrome.** DoorDash blocked headless and required `channel:'chrome'` headed mode — the Akamai/Cloudflare workaround from prior batches (adobe/salesforce) held.
4. **0px-radius = engineering signal.** Databricks (0px buttons, pill tabs only on dark sections) and Squarespace (0px buttons, monochrome) use sharp geometry as a precision/editorial cue — mirroring the automotive monochrome pattern seen in the KR batch.

## Known shared limitation

US consumer apps (DoorDash, Instacart, Snapchat, Reddit) expose rich marketing/web surfaces but their core transactional/feed UI sits behind auth or in native clients — §4 component depth is the marketing+logged-out layer plus (where present) the public DS portal. This is a far smaller gap than the KR/TW app-first brands. Process note: for US brands, always probe `<brand>.design` / `canvas.<brand>.com` / `brand.<brand>.com` first — 4/10 had a token-authoritative portal that beats DOM inference.
