Karrot (당근 — formerly Daangn Market) is the marketplace app every Korean has installed. 30+ million users in a country of 51 million. The product surface is **calm to the point of plain** — no gradient hero, no overlapping cards, no contextual carousels. The system is engineered to feel **trustworthy between strangers** in a category (used goods, neighbor lending) where trust is the entire product.

This is the third post in our series. We pulled tokens from Karrot's [Seed Design](https://seed-design.io) (their open-source DS, second-mover after Bezier) and cross-referenced against live screens.

> **TL;DR** — Karrot's restraint is the strategy. The orange does one job (CTA + active state). The whitespace makes content the product. The platform fonts win over a custom one. Nothing is fighting for attention because everything trusts you'll find what you need.

---

## 1. The atmosphere — neighborhood bulletin board, digitized

Karrot opens on a white canvas (`#ffffff`) with warm near-black headings (`#1a1c20` — `gray-1000` in Seed parlance), generous whitespace, and **one** brand accent: **Karrot Orange `#ff6600`**. That's it. The system reads less like an app and more like a freshly-painted community bulletin board.

The Seed Design system itself is the second mature open-source KR SaaS DS (after [Bezier](./channel-talk-bezier.md)). It ships an 11-step grey scale (`gray-00` → `gray-1000`), a three-tier token architecture (palette → semantic → component), and **full dark mode via semantic token remapping** (not naive inversion). The marketplace category is unusual for shipping dark mode at all — most KR commerce apps don't bother — and Karrot's dark mode is genuinely well-tuned because the semantic layer carries the work.

What makes Karrot visually distinctive is what it **doesn't** do. There's no custom typeface (Pretendard on web, system fonts on native). No drop shadows on cards (depth is 1px gray-200 border). No animated hero. The chrome stays out of the way so user-generated content — photos of used couches, kids' bikes, snacks — becomes the visual experience.

## 2. The five captured insights

### Insight 1 — One color, one job
Karrot Orange (`#ff6600`) appears in exactly two contexts in the product chrome:
1. **Primary CTAs** (buy buttons, message-seller buttons)
2. **Active states** (selected tab, active filter chip)

Never as a card background. Never as a section divider. Never as an icon fill. Compare to Toss (which budgets blue to 2 elements per screen) — Karrot is even stricter, with semantic-only orange. The result: when you see orange, you know it's actionable. Click affordance is **color**, not shape.

### Insight 2 — Platform fonts, no custom face
Most Korean apps with $5M+ design budgets eventually commission a custom typeface (Toss Product Sans, Spoqa Han Sans for Coupang, KB Sans for KB Financial). Karrot doesn't. The stack is **Pretendard Variable** on web, **Apple SD Gothic Neo / Noto Sans CJK KR** on native.

This is a brand decision masquerading as a typography decision. By using platform fonts, Karrot signals "we're like you — we use what you'd use." The product is for neighbors, and neighbors don't license typefaces.

The trade-off: less visual distinctiveness vs Toss/Kakao. The win: zero font-load jank, zero licensing cost, and a brand voice that scales from low-end Android phones (where custom fonts fall back ugly) to flagship iPhones consistently.

### Insight 3 — 4px grid, militantly enforced
The spacing scale is `2px, 4px, 8px, 12px, 16px, 20px, 24px, 32px, 40px, 48px, 64px`. Every value is a multiple of 4. There is no `15px`, no `22px`, no `36px`. This is unusually disciplined for Korean apps which often inherit ad-hoc spacing from web heritage (`14px`, `18px` etc.).

The 4px grid means: any designer or developer who guesses a value will pick the right one 90% of the time. Code reviews skip the "is this 14px or 16px?" conversation entirely.

### Insight 4 — Three shadow levels, both light and dark mode separately tuned
```
shadow-1: 0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)    (light) / 0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,0.32)    (dark)
shadow-2: 0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.12)    (light) / 0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.48)    (dark)
shadow-3: 0 4px 16px rgba(0,0,0,0.16)   (light) / 0 4px 16px rgba(0,0,0,0.64)   (dark)
```

Shadows in light mode are subtle warmth. Shadows in dark mode are **deep darkness** (3-4x alpha) — necessary because dark-on-dark shadows otherwise read as smudges. Most teams ship light shadows and assume dark mode will handle itself. Karrot tuned both.

### Insight 5 — `clamp()`-based accessibility font scaling
Body font size: `clamp(14px, 1rem, 18px)`. This means:
- Default browser → 16px
- User zooms to 200% → caps at 18px (not the runaway 32px)
- User shrinks to 50% → floors at 14px (still readable)

The scaling is **bounded** intentionally. Karrot's product depends on dense neighborhood feeds, so unbounded scaling would break the layout. But the lower and upper bounds preserve accessibility without sacrificing density. Every responsive type token uses this pattern.

## 3. What to steal

1. **One color, one semantic job.** If your brand accent does more than 2 things, you don't have a brand accent — you have decoration.
2. **Platform fonts are a valid brand choice.** Custom typeface is not a prerequisite for "designed-looking." If your product is community-facing, platform fonts may be the *correct* choice.
3. **Pick a grid (4px or 8px) and refuse the off-grid values.** The discipline is the design move.
4. **Tune shadows separately for light/dark mode.** It's 30 minutes of work and the difference is night-and-day (literally).
5. **Use `clamp()` for typography accessibility scaling.** Bounded scaling preserves layout integrity while honoring user preference.

## 4. What NOT to steal

1. **Don't copy Karrot Orange directly.** `#ff6600` is locked to Karrot in Korean visual memory. Drift it to `#FF7E36` or `#E55600` if you want a similar feel without the knockoff association.
2. **Don't import the marketplace pattern wholesale.** Karrot's restraint works because it's a marketplace where content IS the product. If you're a SaaS dashboard, your chrome needs to do more.
3. **Don't ship "trust" through restraint alone.** Karrot earned the "trustworthy strangers" position through dispute resolution, identity verification, and time. UI is the symptom, not the cause.

### 한국어 요약

당근의 UI가 "친근하게" 느껴지는 이유는 디자인의 **억제력** 때문이다.

1. **하나의 색, 하나의 역할** — 당근 오렌지는 CTA + 활성 상태에만. 카드 bg, 섹션 divider, 아이콘 fill에 안 씀.
2. **플랫폼 폰트 사용** — 커스텀 typeface 없음. Pretendard / Apple SD Gothic Neo. "이웃처럼" 사용하는 브랜드 선언.
3. **4px 그리드 엄격 준수** — 15px도, 18px도, 22px도 없음. 모든 spacing이 4의 배수.
4. **다크모드를 의미 토큰 remap으로** — 단순 색반전 아님. light/dark 각각 shadow 별도 튜닝.
5. **`clamp()` 기반 접근성 스케일링** — 14px floor / 18px ceiling. 무제한 zoom 안 됨.
