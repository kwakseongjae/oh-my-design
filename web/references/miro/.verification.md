## Official context and design evidence

- https://miro.com/about/ — first-party origin: RealtimeBoard began in 2011 to help a design agency communicate with distant clients; Miro frames its mission as empowering teams to create the next big thing and its workspace as a place to work with AI across the innovation lifecycle.
- https://miro.com/newsroom/miro-puts-ai-where-teams-work/ — first-party 2025 product evolution: Miro describes an AI Innovation Workspace and keeps the canvas as the shared surface for team work.
- https://miro.com/blog/miro-vis/ — first-party 2023 identity rationale: yellow is iconic but intentionally used with moderation; the system spans colors, shapes, buttons, strokes, illustration, typography, and animation.
- https://miro.com/aura/ — first-party current design/product story: a richer palette is described by color family, and Noto Sans is named as the body-text face for multilingual teams.
- https://developers.miro.com/docs/design-guidelines — first-party developer guidance: Mirotone is Miro’s base CSS component library for apps on the platform, and Miro recommends it to align third-party apps with the product UI. It is documentation/app-library context, not direct evidence for the public web components above.
- https://miro.com/accessibility/ — first-party accessibility commitments: keyboard navigation, named color-picker values, screen-reader/voice-control editing, and reduced motion.
- https://displaay.net/typeface/roobert and https://displaay.net/help/licenses — official foundry/typeface and licence context for Roobert; no Miro redistribution licence is inferred.
