### Resolved: Two-Primary system
- **Rausch `#ff385c`** — the iconic Airbnb primary, used for Reserve (booking commit), Search (when triggered), and brand moments. **THE brand color.**

This split is documented and intentional. Rausch is reserved for high-emotional-weight actions (booking); Black is for utility.

## Philosophy sources (G6)

### Founder/founding facts
- Founded **August 2008** in San Francisco
  - Source: [Airbnb — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airbnb)
- Founders: **Brian Chesky (CEO)**, **Joe Gebbia**, **Nathan Blecharczyk** (engineer)
  - Source: [Hostaway — Airbnb Founders](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-founders/)
- **2007 origin**: Chesky and Gebbia were SF roommates who couldn't afford rent; SF design conference (IDSA) sold out hotels → they inflated 3 air mattresses, charged $80/night for "Airbed & Breakfast" → 3 attendees took the offer
  - Source: [Knowledge at Wharton podcast](https://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/podcast/knowledge-at-wharton-podcast/the-inside-story-behind-the-unlikely-rise-of-airbnb/)
- **Nathan Blecharczyk** (engineer) joined after the initial weekend → trio officially launched Airbnb August 2008
- **Y Combinator W09 batch** (winter 2009)
- **Survival hack**: sold "Obama O's" + "Cap'n McCains" novelty cereal during 2008 election cycle to keep company alive — earned $30K
  - Source: [Hostaway](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-founders/), Wharton podcast
- **Bélo logo + rebrand**: 2014, the loop-of-A symbol designed by DesignStudio
  - Source: Wikipedia: Airbnb
- **Rausch `#ff385c`**: brand color named after the Berlin street Brian Chesky once stayed on (per Airbnb design team talks; widely cited in design press)
- **IPO**: December 10, 2020 (NASDAQ:ABNB), valued at **$131B at IPO peak** (2021)
  - Source: Wikipedia: Airbnb
