### Single-system: 99999px Pill + display-p3 Orange-Red + ALL CAPS

Sanity uses a **wide-gamut chrome with all-pill discipline**:
- All CTAs: **99999px full-pill** (no exceptions)
- **Primary `color(display-p3 1 0.3333 0)`** — wide-gamut Orange-Red (~`#ff5500` sRGB) — **Sanity is the most modern color-space adopter in the corpus**, using **CSS Color Module Level 4 `display-p3()`** rather than just `lab()`/`oklab()` (Cursor/Lovable/Mintlify) or `oklab()` (Mistral)
- Inverse `#0b0b0b` Near-Black (canvas-aware)
- Light secondary `#ededed` (subtle off-white)
- A11y skip-nav `#0052ef` Sanity Blue (utility-only)
- Most CTAs **35px / 13px·400 ALL CAPS** (distinctive uppercase canonical chrome)
- Hero exception: 55px / **24px·400** (larger size for hero "Start building")
- Code-tab: weight 600 (different — tab-indicator register)

The `color(display-p3 1 0.3333 0)` syntax means Sanity is rendering the brand orange in **wide-gamut P3 color space** on supported displays — appearing more vivid on M-series Macs / iPhones than the sRGB `#ff5500` fallback. This is **the most advanced color-space technique in the corpus** (more modern than Mistral's oklab).

This corrects the prior footer's "black `#0b0b0b` 0px / 6×0 / 36px / 16px·400" claim — that was a **announcement banner pill** (`How AI is powering...`), not the canonical Primary. The canonical Primary is **`color(display-p3 1 0.3333 0)` Sanity Orange-Red 99999px** — completely different.
