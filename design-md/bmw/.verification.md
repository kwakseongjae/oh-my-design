## Philosophy sources (G6)

### Founder/founding facts
- **Bayerische Flugzeugwerke (BFW) founded March 7, 1916** — aircraft producer, formerly Otto Flugmaschinenfabrik, in Munich
  - Source: [BMW — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMW), [History of BMW — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_BMW)
- **Renamed Bayerische Motoren Werke (BMW) in 1922** — moniker first used 1917 when Rapp Motorenwerke became BMW GmbH
  - Source: Wikipedia
- **Founders' lineage:** **Karl Rapp** + **Gustav Otto** (Otto Flugmaschinenfabrik / Rapp Motorenwerke roots)
  - Source: [BMW Group History](https://www.bmwgroup.com/en/company/history.html)
- **First product:** BMW IIIa aircraft engine — known for fuel economy + high-altitude performance
  - Source: [PBS — What BMW did before luxury cars](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/heres-what-bmw-did-before-it-made-luxury-cars)
- **Treaty of Versailles 1918**: BMW forced to cease aircraft engine production. Pivoted to **farm equipment, household items, railway brakes** to survive
  - Source: PBS
- **1923**: motorcycle production begins (R32, the first BMW motorcycle)
- **1928**: automobile market entry (Dixi acquisition)
- **2024-2025**: Neue Klasse EV launch — BMW i7 / iX flagship EVs; AI Assistant integrated into web + product
- **Brand tagline:** *"Sheer Driving Pleasure"* / *"Freude am Fahren"* — long-standing positioning
- **Roundel logo origin:** white + blue quarters represent **Bavarian flag** (white sky, blue diamonds) — NOT a propeller (despite popular myth, debunked by BMW corporate communications)
