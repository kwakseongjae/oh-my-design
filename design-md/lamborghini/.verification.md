### Single-system: ALL CAPS 18px·400 ghost chrome on total-darkness canvas

Lamborghini, like Ferrari, uses an **imagery-led no-button discipline** — the page is a cinematic film reel, and the chrome is reduced to ALL CAPS text-only labels on transparent black:
- All nav: 18px·400 ALL CAPS, no fills, no radii — the imagery carries everything
- 0px sharp radius globally (Italian-precision shared with Ferrari)
- Cookie banner is the only filled CTA pattern, and it's a utility track (GDPR), not the canonical brand chrome
- No discoverable Primary CTA on home or /models in standard `a/button` queries — the brand operates **entirely on hover-revealed image overlays + arrow-link affordances**, not buttons

**This is intentional positioning**: Lamborghini doesn't sell on a "Get started" button. The supercar register is browse → contact dealership IRL, so the website doesn't need a high-affordance CTA system. The chrome is there to **not interfere with the imagery**.

This corrects the prior footer's "cookie CTAs / 2px / 13.008px·600" claim — that was the GDPR utility, not the brand canon. The actual brand canon is **ALL CAPS 18px·400 ghost text on `#000` canvas**.

## Philosophy sources (G6)

### Founder/founding facts
- **Automobili Ferruccio Lamborghini S.p.A. founded 1963** at **Sant'Agata Bolognese**
  - Source: [Lamborghini — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lamborghini)
  - Source: [Lamborghini.com — History/Company](https://www.lamborghini.com/en-en/history/company)
- Founder: **Ferruccio Lamborghini** — Italian manufacturing magnate; ran **Lamborghini Trattori** (tractor business) before founding the car company
  - Source: [Ferruccio Lamborghini — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferruccio_Lamborghini)
- **Founding engineering team**: Giotto Bizzarrini (engine) + Gian Paolo Dallara + Paolo Stanzani (chassis) + Franco Scaglione (bodywork)
- **First series-produced car**: **350 GT** (V-12 grand tourer)
- **1973 oil crisis** → sales decline → **1972 majority stake sold to Georges-Henri Rossetti** → **1974 remaining interest sold to René Leimer**, Ferruccio retired
- **1998: Mycom Setdco + V'Power sold Lamborghini to Volkswagen Group** — placed under **Audi** control
  - Source: [Volkswagen Group — History of Lamborghini](https://www.volkswagen-group.com/en/the-history-of-the-brands-17668/the-history-of-lamborghini-17676)
- **Bull theme**: Aventador, Huracán, Urus, Revuelto — names from Spanish fighting-bull lineage; Ferruccio was Taurus zodiac
- **Famous Ferrari-clutch dispute origin** is **apocryphal but widely circulated** — should not be presented as verified fact
